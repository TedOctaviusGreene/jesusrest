Our Method

Let the Bible interpret the Bible.

We don’t build doctrine on tradition, a teacher’s opinion, or a feeling. We let Scripture explain Scripture — by the Bible’s own rules. Here they are, plainly.

Why this matters

Nearly every division and error in the church traces back to the same root: a single verse lifted out, then explained by something outside the Bible — a denomination’s history, a charismatic teacher, or a personal impression. Scripture forbids exactly that.

“…no prophecy of the scripture is of any private interpretation.” — 2 Peter 1:20

So we read the Bible by the Bible. Below are the rules it gives for understanding itself.

The Rules

Six rules the Bible gives for reading the Bible

1

No private interpretation

No verse is yours to bend. Its meaning is fixed by the Author, not the reader.

2 Peter 1:20
2

Compare Scripture with Scripture

Spiritual truth is understood by comparing spiritual with spiritual — line upon line, verse with verse.

1 Cor 2:13 · Isaiah 28:10
3

Let the clear explain the unclear

Build on the plain passages, and let them settle the hard or symbolic ones — never the reverse.

Deut 29:29
4

Honor the context

Read each verse in its sentence, chapter, and the whole counsel of God — not as an isolated fragment.

Acts 20:27
5

Let the New interpret the Old

The apostles show us how to read the Old Testament — as shadow fulfilled in Christ.

Luke 24:27 · Col 2:17
6

Define words the Bible’s way

Let Scripture define its own terms — “law,” “rest,” “works,” “sin” — by how it uses them, not by culture.

1 Cor 2:13
