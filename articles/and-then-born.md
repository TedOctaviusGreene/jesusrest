# And Then, Born

*The end the whole system is built toward — the begotten son or daughter of God, finally born into glory and made fully like Christ.*

**The Salvation System — Part 8 of Eight**

This article is about the last part of a single, connected system of salvation, and it answers the question every other part has been pointing toward: what is the whole system finally for? Here is the whole system in one sentence, so that this last part can be seen in its place: God's salvation is a gift that a person cannot earn and cannot lose; the person who receives that gift becomes a son or a daughter of God; a son or daughter who lives by the flesh is disciplined but never disowned; a son or daughter who yields to Christ bears Christ's own fruit and is rewarded for it; and at no point in the entire system can anyone boast.

Everything in the system has been moving toward one moment. A son or daughter of God has been carried, kept, corrected, and grown — begotten, but not yet born. This final article tells what happens when the waiting ends. The son or daughter of God is at last delivered — born into glory — and made fully like the One whose life has been inside the whole time.

## The birth the whole system was for

The day the waiting ends, the change is total:

> So also is the resurrection of the dead. It is sown in corruption; it is raised in incorruption… it is sown a natural body; it is raised a spiritual body… and as we have borne the image of the earthy, we shall also bear the image of the heavenly.
>
> — 1 Corinthians 15:42–44, 49 KJV

> When He shall appear, we shall be like Him; for we shall see Him as He is.
>
> — 1 John 3:2 KJV

That is the birth the whole system has been building toward. There is no more war between the flesh and the Spirit. There is no more falling into the very thing a son or daughter of God hates. The struggling, divided person is at last made fully like Christ, sharing Christ's life completely and without rivalry. The womb of this present life gives way to the open day of Christ's own likeness. That is the moment a son or daughter of God is truly born — brought all the way into the family at last, the begotten child finally delivered.

---

## So hear it plainly

So hear it plainly, every reader who has been afraid. A person who has received Christ is not on trial for their life. That person is a son or a daughter of God — begotten, kept, and carried toward glory by a Father who cannot deny Himself. That son or daughter will be disciplined when they wander, because they are loved, and will never be cast out, because they belong to God. A son or daughter of God does not have to manufacture a holy life by clenching their fists and trying harder. A son or daughter of God has only to yield — to stay joined to Christ, to let Christ live, and to watch Christ bear His own fruit through them for His own glory.

This is the whole system, and it stands as one piece. Salvation is the gift, held by faith and never lost. Discipline is the Father's hand on a son or daughter who wanders, never the world's sentence on a stranger. Reward is the crown gathered by a yielded life. Faith receives the righteousness; the works of that faith gather the reward; and a faith that does nothing gathers only discipline. And glory is the end of it all, when the begotten son or daughter is finally born into the likeness of Christ. The system is fair, because each son or daughter of God is weighed by what they let Christ do through them. The system is loving, because no son or daughter of God is ever lost. And the system is humbling, because from the first gift to the final crown, there was never one moment that belonged to anyone to boast about. It was Christ, from beginning to end.
