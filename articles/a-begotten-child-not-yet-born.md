# A Saved Person Is a Begotten Child, Not Yet Born

*What the gift of salvation actually makes you — a true son or daughter of God now, not yet born into glory.*

**The Salvation System — Part 2 of Eight**

This article is about one part of a single, connected system of salvation, and it answers a plain question: what kind of person does the gift of salvation actually make you? Here is the whole system in one sentence, so that this part can be seen in its place: God's salvation is a gift that a person cannot earn and cannot lose; the person who receives that gift becomes a son or a daughter of God; a son or daughter who lives by the flesh is disciplined but never disowned; a son or daughter who yields to Christ bears Christ's own fruit and is rewarded for it; and at no point in the entire system can anyone boast.

The first part of the system is that salvation is a gift, received by faith, and impossible to earn or to lose. This article takes the next step and shows what that gift does to the person who receives it. The moment a person believes, that person is not merely forgiven and then left standing outside as a stranger. That person becomes a child of God — a son or a daughter — by being given God's own life. And Scripture describes this new standing with one careful word that is easy to misread. Scripture says the believer is *begotten*.

## Begotten is not the same as born

The word Peter uses is begotten:

> Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, which according to His abundant mercy hath begotten us again unto a lively hope by the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead.
>
> — 1 Peter 1:3 KJV

The word *begotten* is deliberate, and it matters. In Scripture the same family of language covers both the begetting and the bearing — the seed conceived and the child delivered. People tend to flatten all of it into the single phrase "born again," and so they miss what is really being said. A child is begotten long before that child is born. There is a true, living, growing person in the womb who already belongs to the family and is already loved — and that person is still not yet delivered.

That is exactly where a son or daughter of God stands right now. The seed of God is already inside the believer, and it stays there:

> Whosoever is born of God doth not commit sin; for His seed remaineth in him.
>
> — 1 John 3:9 KJV

And yet the believer's delivery — the birth into glory — is plainly spoken of as still future, as something the son or daughter of God is presently waiting for:

> And not only they, but ourselves also, which have the firstfruits of the Spirit, even we ourselves groan within ourselves, waiting for the adoption, to wit, the redemption of our body.
>
> — Romans 8:23 KJV

The believer already *has* the firstfruits of the Spirit — that is the begetting, and it is already done. And yet the believer is still *waiting* for the adoption, which the verse itself defines as the redemption of the body. The child is conceived and alive. The child is not yet born. That is why John can hold both halves together in one sentence without any strain:

> Beloved, now are we the sons of God, and it doth not yet appear what we shall be: but we know that, when He shall appear, we shall be like Him; for we shall see Him as He is.
>
> — 1 John 3:2 KJV

*Now* a believer is a son or daughter of God — settled, present tense, beyond question. And *not yet* has it appeared what that son or daughter will become. The believer's place in the family is already finished; the believer's glory is still on the way. A son or daughter of God is begotten, not yet born.

> **A child in the womb is already yours. You are not less His because you are not yet born.**

## Why this answers the fear

This settled standing answers a fear directly. A person does not become a son or daughter of God by performing well enough to qualify for the family. A person who has received the gift already *is* a son or daughter of God. Nothing in the womb earns its way into the family; the child in the womb is simply, already, alive in it.

And this settled standing is what makes the next part of the system possible. Because a believer is already a true son or daughter of God, and not a stranger on trial, what God does when that son or daughter sins is the work of a Father with His own child, not the work of a judge with a defendant. What that Father does with a wandering son or daughter is the subject of the next article.
