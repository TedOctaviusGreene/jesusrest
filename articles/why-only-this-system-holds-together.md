# Why Only This System Holds Together

*The test any true system must pass — every promise keeps its place, every warning keeps its force, and not one verse has to be bent.*

**The Salvation System — Part 7 of Eight**

This article is about one part of a single, connected system of salvation, and it asks the question that decides whether the whole system is true: of all the ways people try to fit these verses together, why does only this one hold? Here is the whole system in one sentence, so that this part can be seen in its place: God's salvation is a gift that a person cannot earn and cannot lose; the person who receives that gift becomes a son or a daughter of God; a son or daughter who lives by the flesh is disciplined but never disowned; a son or daughter who yields to Christ bears Christ's own fruit and is rewarded for it; and at no point in the entire system can anyone boast.

Every part of the system has now been shown on its own. This article does something different. This article sets all the parts side by side and applies the test that any true system has to pass: does the system let every verse keep its own meaning, or does it have to bend some verses in order to make the others fit? This article shows that the system bends nothing. Every promise keeps its place, every warning keeps its force, and the whole thing stands together as one piece.

## Set the parts side by side

Set the parts of the system next to one another and look at how each one supports the rest. The gift cannot be earned, so no one can be proud. The gift cannot be lost, so no one needs to live in fear. The son or daughter of God is a real child, so the wandering of that son or daughter is met with a Father's discipline and not a stranger's rejection. The fruit of a holy life is Christ's own life and not the believer's striving, so even holiness gives a son or daughter of God nothing to boast about. The reward is justly weighed, and the crown is truly offered and truly able to be missed, so the warnings keep their full force without ever touching the gift. And the glory at the end is certain, because the begotten son or daughter will finally be born into the likeness of Christ Himself.

That is the test of a true system: it lets every verse keep its own meaning. The terrifying warnings stay terrifying, because the warnings are aimed at the reward, where they belong. The unshakable promises stay unshakable, because the promises are aimed at the gift, where they belong. James stays true and Paul stays true, because each man is heard speaking to his own readers about his own subject. Nothing has to be explained away. And the whole system lands exactly where God said it would land, with every mouth stopped:

> That no flesh should glory in His presence… That, according as it is written, He that glorieth, let him glory in the Lord.
>
> — 1 Corinthians 1:29, 31 KJV

## What every other arrangement gets wrong

Now test the alternatives against that same standard. A system a person could lose would force that person to live in fear. A system a person could earn would give that person room to boast. A system with no discipline would make being a son or daughter of God meaningless. A system with no reward would make a yielded life pointless. Each of those arrangements has to break some verse to hold itself together: it either softens a promise to make room for fear, or it softens a warning to make room for pride.

Only this system is at once unshakable and serious, free and accountable, tender and just. And only this system leaves no one with anything to boast about except the Lord Himself. The system is fair. The system is loving. And no one can boast. That is not the fingerprint of human beings. That is the fingerprint of God.

> **The warnings stay terrifying. The promises stay unshakable. Nothing has to be explained away.**

## The system as one piece

This is the system, and it stands as one piece. Salvation is the gift — held by faith, never by works, and so never lost. Discipline is the Father's hand on a son or daughter who wanders, never the world's sentence on a stranger. Reward is the crown gathered by the yielded life, where Christ was let in to bear His fruit. Faith receives the righteousness; the works of that faith gather the reward; and a faith that produces nothing gathers only discipline. Pull any single part out, and the rest collapse into either fear or pride. Hold all the parts together, and the whole thing stands.

One part of the system still waits to be told in full — the end the whole thing is built toward. That end is the subject of the final article.
