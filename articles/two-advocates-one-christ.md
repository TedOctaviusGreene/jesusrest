# Two Advocates, One Christ

*He came to us. Then He came into us. Both are rest.*

There is a moment in John 14 that most readers pass over too quickly. Jesus has just told His disciples He is leaving. The room fills with the kind of dread that only comes when the one person holding everything together announces He will not be there anymore. Then He says something that should stop us cold.

*"I will ask the Father, and He will give you another Advocate."*

Another. The word itself implies there is already one. And there is. It is Him. Jesus, standing in that upper room, has been their Advocate from the beginning — arguing their case before heaven, bearing their sin in His own body, finishing the work the Law could never finish. He is the first Advocate. And now He is telling them a second is coming.

Except — and this is where the Greek opens something extraordinary — the word He uses for "another" is *allos*. Not *heteros*, which means another of a different kind. *Allos.* Another of the exact same kind. Same nature. Same presence. Same Christ.

Two Advocates. One Person. Two modes of the same infinite love.

## The First Advocate: *Christ Beside You*

For three years, the disciples had something no human being had ever had: God with skin on, walking the same roads, eating at the same tables, sleeping in the same storms. When Peter sank, a hand pulled him up. When Thomas doubted, wounds were offered to be touched. When a woman caught in adultery was thrown to the ground, He knelt beside her in the dirt.

This is the first mode of advocacy — embodied, present, local. Jesus as *Paraklētos* in flesh: the One called to stand beside you, to speak in your defense, to bear what you cannot bear. And what He bore in that body was total. Every sin. Every accusation. Every debt the Law had recorded against you. He took it into the cross and He left it there.

> Debt canceled. Case closed. Done. Your standing before God was never your project — it was His. And He finished it.
> — John 19:30 — Tetelestai

*"It is finished."* Three words in English. One word in Greek — *tetelestai* — the very term stamped across paid bills in the ancient world. Paid in full. The first Advocate rested His case, and the Judge of all the earth agreed. Your standing before God is not your project. It was His — and He finished it.

This is the first rest. Not rest as in sleep, but rest as in *settlement.* The verdict is in. You do not have to earn what He already earned. You do not have to complete what He already completed. Rest in what Christ did.

## The Problem*with the Body*

Here is what we rarely sit with: Jesus in a body was Jesus in one place.

He could be in Capernaum or Jerusalem — not both. He could speak to twelve men in an upper room, but not simultaneously to a shepherd in Ethiopia, a merchant in Rome, a fisherman in Corinth. The Incarnation was the most glorious thing that had ever happened to the created order, but it was also, by design, contained. Geographically limited. Temporally bound to one generation, one nation, one brief window in history.

Which is exactly why He said what He said in John 16:7.

*"It is to your advantage that I go away."*

The disciples heard loss. It was the opposite. Jesus was describing an upgrade so vast they could not yet hold it. If He stayed in the body, He could be with some of them. If He went — if He passed through death and rose and ascended — He could be with *all* of them. All of us. In every age. In every nation. Not beside us. *Inside* us.

This is not a lesser presence. It is a greater one. The container of flesh was never the final goal. The goal was always the interior — always *Christ in you* — and the body was the pathway to get there. He had to go through the cross to get out of the local and into the universal. He had to complete the work of the first Advocate so He could arrive as the second.

## The Second Advocate: *Christ Within You*

Three verses after Jesus promises "another Advocate," He says something that should shatter every notion of the Spirit as a third-party helper:

*"I will come to you."*

Not "He will come." Not "the Spirit will arrive." *I.* Jesus. The same One standing in that room announcing that He is the One coming — not to stand beside them anymore, but to take up residence within them. The second Advocate is not a replacement. He is Christ Himself, freed from the limitation of one body, now fully present to every person who receives Him.

Paul understood this so completely that he moves in Romans 8 without even a breath between "the Spirit of Christ" and "Christ in you." They are the same sentence. The Spirit is how Christ inhabits you. And if Christ inhabits you — the full, risen, unlimited Christ — then what exactly is He doing in there?

He is not sitting quietly, waiting to assist your effort. He is living. He is the Vine, and you are the branch, and the branch does not strain to produce fruit. The branch *rests* in the Vine. The life flows from the root. The fruit appears — not because the branch worked harder, but because the Vine is alive and the branch stayed connected.

## Two Rests,*One Gospel*

Most of Christianity has learned, at least in principle, to rest in the first. We understand that salvation is not earned — that the cross is enough, that grace is real, that our standing before God is settled. We know the first rest, even if we forget it on hard days.

But the second rest remains largely undiscovered. We believe Christ saved us and then left us to manage the Christian life with Spirit-assistance. We have reduced the second Advocate to a coach who cheers us on, a helper who boosts our spiritual performance, a power source we tap when we run low. And so we labor. We strive. We produce — or fail to produce — and we carry the weight of both outcomes.

But this is not what the second Advocate is. He is not a supplement to your effort. He is the *end* of your effort as the source. "Christ in you, the hope of glory" — not Christ helping you, not Christ near you, but Christ as the living reality within you, bearing fruit through your branches for His own glory, which He shares with you freely.

> The first rest says: **you don't have to pay what He already paid.**  
> The second rest says: **you don't have to do what He is already doing.**  
> Both are grace. Both are gift. Both are the same Christ.

First, the Advocate who stood in your place in history — bearing every charge, satisfying every claim, settling every debt with His own life. Then, the Advocate who lives in your place right now — producing every fruit, fulfilling every command, loving through you with a love that was never yours to manufacture.

He finished the work of salvation so you could rest in it. He rose and came to live inside you so you could rest *in Him.*

Two Advocates. One Christ. Infinite love. Total rest.

> "Christ in you, the hope of glory."
> — Colossians 1:27
