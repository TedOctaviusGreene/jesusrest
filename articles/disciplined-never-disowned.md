# A Son or Daughter Is Disciplined, Never Disowned

*What the system does with a child of God who still sins — correction from a Father, never rejection by a judge.*

**The Salvation System — Part 3 of Eight**

This article is about one part of a single, connected system of salvation, and it answers a question every honest believer eventually asks: what does God do with a son or daughter who still sins? Here is the whole system in one sentence, so that this part can be seen in its place: God's salvation is a gift that a person cannot earn and cannot lose; the person who receives that gift becomes a son or a daughter of God; a son or daughter who lives by the flesh is disciplined but never disowned; a son or daughter who yields to Christ bears Christ's own fruit and is rewarded for it; and at no point in the entire system can anyone boast.

The system has already established two things. Salvation is a gift that cannot be lost, and the person who receives it is a true son or daughter of God. This article takes the next step. Every son or daughter of God will, at times, live by self-will and by the flesh. The question the system has to answer is what God does about it. The answer is that God disciplines a wandering son or daughter — God corrects the child — but God never disowns the child and never casts the child out.

## Discipline is the proof, not the threat

This is where many sincere people lose their footing, so let it be said plainly. The fact that a believer still sins does not threaten that believer's salvation. But the sin is not nothing, either. A real Father does something about a wandering child, and what the Father does is correct the child rather than throw the child out:

> My son, despise not thou the chastening of the Lord, nor faint when thou art rebuked of Him: for whom the Lord loveth He chasteneth, and scourgeth every son whom He receiveth… But if ye be without chastisement, whereof all are partakers, then are ye bastards, and not sons.
>
> — Hebrews 12:5–6, 8 KJV

Read that passage backward, and the whole fear turns over. Discipline is not evidence that a person might not belong to God. Discipline is the proof that the person does belong to God. The one whom God leaves entirely alone is the one with no place in the family at all. If the Father's correction is reaching a son or daughter, it is because the Father Himself is reaching that son or daughter. Paul says exactly the same thing about believers who had grown careless:

> But when we are judged, we are chastened of the Lord, that we should not be condemned with the world.
>
> — 1 Corinthians 11:32 KJV

Read it closely: a believer is chastened so that the believer will not be condemned with the world. The chastening is the very thing that marks a son or daughter of God off from the world's judgment. A son or daughter of God receives the Father's correction precisely *instead of* the world's condemnation. That is the line that separates a son or daughter from a stranger.

## The discipline is real and can be painful

None of this is small. Living by self-will and by the flesh — and every son or daughter of God will do it, just as Paul confessed that he kept doing the very thing he hated (Romans 7:15–19) — carries real weight. Scripture does not soften it:

> It is a fearful thing to fall into the hands of the living God.
>
> — Hebrews 10:31 KJV

> Be not deceived; God is not mocked: for whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap.
>
> — Galatians 6:7 KJV

So two things are true at the same time, and the system needs both of them. A son or daughter of God cannot lose salvation — and a son or daughter of God can still be hurt, corrected, and made to reap a hard harvest. The discipline can be painful, and it is meant to be. But the discipline falls on a child the Father will not let go. The Father wounds in order to keep the child, never in order to discard the child.

> **The Father wounds in order to keep a son or daughter, never in order to discard them.**

## Safe and serious at once

This is what makes the system both safe and serious at the same time. A son or daughter of God is safe, because the gift of salvation cannot be lost. A son or daughter of God is also accountable, because a Father disciplines the children He loves. Neither truth cancels the other one.

And this raises the next question the system has to answer. If a son or daughter of God is disciplined for living by the flesh, then how does a son or daughter of God live any other way? The answer is not greater effort and not a tighter grip. The answer is the next part of the system, and it is the part most people miss.
